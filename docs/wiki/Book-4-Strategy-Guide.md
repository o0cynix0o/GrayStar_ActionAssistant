# Book 4 Strategy Guide — *War of the Wizards*

> *"Hail Wizard, son of man. You have succeeded. That which you hold in your hand contains the power to defeat the evil Wytch-king, Shasarak."*
>
> — Acarya, High Wizard of the Shianti, Section 1

This is the finale. The Moonstone is in your hand. The voices of the Shianti are in your head — Acarya, then Maiteya himself, your old teacher, telling you with that affectionate *"little one"* that seven years have passed in the material world while you were gone. Sado is in Karnali leading an army. The Freedom Guild has held a siege and grown into something real. The Shadakine Empire is starting to crack. And somewhere beyond all of that, behind a wall of fire, the Wytch-king Shasarak is waiting for the one wizard who can actually finish him.

*War of the Wizards* is not a victory lap. It looks like one, because you finally have power that *matches* the danger — but it asks you to spend that power carefully, keep moving when the plains turn against you, and choose the exact moment when Grey Star finally stops running and faces the wizard who has been hunting him for four books.

A heads-up before we begin: this guide names the major Book 4 set-pieces by name (Lissan Plain, the Demon portal at Tilos, the bridge at Lanzi, Shadaki, Shasarak, Agarash) because you can't really write a strategy guide without them. It avoids dwelling on the *outcomes* of those scenes — those you should meet for the first time in the book itself, not in this article. If you want the spoiler-heavy completionist version of the same advice, that's what the [100% Achievement Guide](Achievement-100-Percent-Guide) is for.

## Quick Start

For your first clear, play like a survivor with a crown jewel in one hand. Keep **Willpower** high. The Moonstone makes you stronger, but Book 4 is *full* of small magical costs, huge magical costs, and a few ugly moments where the book asks whether you can afford the spell you want.

Take practical supplies when the Masbate offer them. A **Sword**, a **Coil of Rope**, a **Tinderbox**, a **Water Bottle**, a **Masbate Battle Horn**. None of it is glamorous. All of it earns its place in the Backpack. Book 4 rewards the player who stays *prepared*, not the player who's been saving everything for one big magical moment.

Use the Assistant for every combat with a preset. Book 4 absolutely loves timed fights — win within three rounds, survive one fly-by pass, or get pushed into a different section because the fight dragged on too long. The Combat tab knows the rules. You should let it.

## What This Book Is Really Testing

Book 4 tests whether you can finish the job without burning yourself out. You are *powerful* now. The Moonstone is real power, and the Higher Magicks give you answers you simply did not have in the earlier books. The trap is thinking every problem should be solved with magic.

The best Book 4 run keeps two questions running in the back of your head:

- Can I solve this without spending Willpower?
- If I spend Willpower here, will I still have enough when Shasarak finally appears?

That's the heart of the book. You're not weak anymore, but you can still arrive at the final confrontation empty — and the book is *very* willing to let you do that and watch what happens.

## Best First Playthrough

Listen to the Shianti at the start, then get moving. On the Lissan Plain, do not wander blindly if you have a safer scouting option — Prophecy and Visionary paths cost Willpower, but they often show you where the danger is *before* you step into it. Cheaper than the alternative.

When you can prepare before the demon portal, prepare. The **Potion of Invulnerability** is one of the strongest tools in the book. If you can brew it and use it at the right time, it can turn a brutal demon stretch into something genuinely manageable. (Many players brew the potion, then hoard it. Don't be that hero. The achievements track the *use* separately from the brew.)

Once the portal business is done, focus on the bigger war. The bridge, the Shadakine uniform, the Masbate, and the Freedom Guild all matter. Book 4 is about making the enemy army collide with the demon threat *before* you march into Shadaki for the final answer — and the only person who can arrange that collision is you.

## Paths Worth Knowing

### The Shianti's Counsel

The opening choices at Section 1 are all useful, and they merge quickly. Ask where you're being sent if you want practical geography. Ask for advice if you want the cleanest strategic nudge. Ask to see Shasarak if you want the mood of the finale right away. None of these choices should lock you out of victory, so they're safe replay flavor.

### Lissan Plain and Phinomel Pods

The Lissan Plain is hot, empty, and *mean*. The Shianti tell you exactly why before they drop you off:

> *"This was once the domain of the Masbaté tribe, warrior nomads of great prowess … The demons still roam the plains, and no one dares go there."*
>
> — Section 348

Read that twice, because it's also a fair description of the *gameplay* on the plain. Resist. Resist. Resist some more. Eventually a demon plague catches up to you and the rules change. If you find the Phinomel plant, the Assistant can handle the roll and add the pods you gather. Those pods become much more interesting later if you reach the Leafwater pool in Fernmost — they can improve your Combat Skill with the Staff, which is a very nice thing to have before Shasarak.

For a clean first run, take the pods if the roll goes your way. For replay, come back and chase the better harvest.

### The Masbate Rescue

The tortured Masbate survivor is one of the book's big turns. This path can lead through traps, demon fights, and finally a reveal that changes the whole shape of the war. You stop being two wanderers on the plain and become part of an army's last chance. It's the most narratively *satisfying* mid-book stretch in Book 4 — well worth the time on a replay even after you know what it leads to.

### Invulnerability Prep

If you can create or use a **Potion of Invulnerability**, do it *thoughtfully*. The potion is strongest when the book is about to throw you into the demon press near Tilos. The Assistant can remove the potion, return the empty vial, and mark the protection state so you don't have to remember it in the middle of a fight.

Thaumaturgy can also give you invulnerability protection, but it costs Willpower. The potion is often the sweeter deal — same effect, paid for in advance with brewing time instead of with magic you'll want at the climax.

### The Demon Portal of Tilos

This is one of the most dangerous sections of the book. The first sign that you've walked into something worse than usual is the artifact in your hand:

> *You glance down at the Moonstone. Shocked, you see that it contains an absolute blackness. It is lifeless and still.*
>
> — Section 81

When the Moonstone goes dark, *the rules are different*. The Moonstone can push back the demon power, but the costs are real. Several portal choices use the special Book 4 Willpower rule that the book spells out plainly when you fire the Staff at a bridge:

> *If you do not have enough WILLPOWER points, you must use ENDURANCE points at a rate of 2 ENDURANCE points for every 1 WILLPOWER point you lack.*
>
> — Section 175

That's the trade. You can pay for a spell with the only currency you have left, and the only currency you have left is your own body. Use the Assistant's WP-cost buttons here. This is *exactly* the bookkeeping the app is built for, and you do not want to be doing this math manually with your Endurance on the line.

### The Shadakine Uniform

The uniform path is one of the cleverer routes in the book. If you can take a Shadakine uniform, you can mislead the enemy and aim the demon horde where it will do the most damage. It is not just a costume change — it's one of the cleanest ways Grey Star turns Shasarak's own war machine against him.

### The Bridge at Lanzi

The bridge sequence is the big battlefield set-piece. Expect expensive magic, hard combat, and timed results. The payoff for getting it right is the kind of cinematic moment Book 4 deals in:

> *The bridge erupts into flames and collapses into the waters of the River Dosar. Then you faint with exhaustion into Samu's arms.*
>
> — Section 252

That's what victory looks like at Lanzi — fire on the water, Samu catching you, and the army on the move toward the south. The **Demon Master** fight is especially important here: winning quickly sends you one way, while lasting too long sends you another. Let the Combat tab handle the round count — eyeballing it is how players miss the fast finish and end up on the wrong route.

### Freedom Guild and Fernmost

Fernmost is your last chance to *breathe*. Rest when the book gives you rest. Take the healing. If you have Alchemy or Theurgy, the late herb cache is excellent — **Potion of Laumspur**, **Potion of Alether**, **Tarama Seeds**, **Temeris**, **Mustow**, and **Ezeran Acid** are all useful depending on the path you take.

If you gathered Phinomel Pods earlier, look for the Leafwater opportunity. A stronger Staff is exactly the sort of final-book reward you want to bring into the closing chapters.

### Shadaki and Shasarak

The final approach can come through teleportation, the Morn Pass, or direct challenge. Once you reach Shasarak, read carefully — there are several ways the duel can unfold. You can fight him directly, use the Moonstone, or end up in a desperate test of Willpower and Endurance.

When the Staff shatters, the Assistant removes it. That's not a bug. That's the story reaching its last page.

### Agarash

Do not let the finale trick you into thinking Shasarak is the only danger. Agarash is the deeper threat behind the wall of fire, and the ending is about stopping both the Wytch-king *and* the disaster waiting behind him. If the book offers you a final chance to close the portal, take the moment seriously. The choice is real.

## Items Worth Chasing

The **Potion of Invulnerability** is the standout. It can protect you through some genuinely awful demon fighting.

**Phinomel Pods** are easy to underestimate. They matter because of Leafwater.

The **Masbate Battle Horn** is a great story item and belongs in any replay where you're exploring the battlefield side of the book.

**Potion of Laumspur (+6 END)** is simple and excellent. Take it when you can.

**Potion of Alether** is a nice pre-fight boost, especially heading into the final stretch.

**Tarama Seeds** are powerful but easy to misuse. Treat each seed like one free spell or one long-range Staff use, not like an infinite combat trick.

**Potion of Temeris** can save Willpower on teleportation choices. That can matter a lot near the end.

## Combat and Rolls

Book 4 has a lot of fights, and many of them are not ordinary. Some care about round count. Some are one-round fly-by attacks. Some let you ignore your Endurance loss if you hurt the enemy more than it hurts you. Some have invulnerability variants. The Assistant now has presets for the detected Book 4 fights, so start combat from the section controls whenever you can.

Use the roll helper too. Book 4 rolls often add current Endurance, current Willpower, both together, or bonuses from special items and Magicks. The roll button keeps the math tidy when the action is fast.

## Common Mistakes

The first mistake is treating the Moonstone like free power. It is *not* free.

The second mistake is ignoring Phinomel Pods because they look like a side item. They can become a Staff boost later — that's a meaningful upgrade for the closing fights.

The third mistake is forgetting that invulnerability is temporary. Use it when the danger is right in front of you, not when you're saving it for a moment that might never come.

The fourth mistake is letting timed combats run without tracking the round count. In Book 4, one extra round can change the section you go to.

The fifth mistake is reaching Shasarak with no Willpower left and hoping the finale will be gentle. It will not.

## Achievement Cleanup

First, finish the book. That should cover **Book Four Complete**, **Moonstone Bearer**, **Staff in Ashes**, **Agarash Defied**, and **Wizard Regent** on a clean final path. Second, replay the Lissan Plain for **Phinomel Harvest** and **Blue-Light Brew**. Third, replay the portal preparation and demon-front sections for **Untouchable For Now**, **Portal Breaker**, and **Bridge at Lanzi**. Fourth, spend a run on the war story: **Masbate Found**, **Borrowed Colors**, **Freedom Guild Reached**, and **Fernmost Respite**. Finally, clean up combat achievements with the Combat tab open — Book 4 has plenty of hard enemies, low-ratio fights, and long round counts for **War-Worn Wizard** and **Last Stand Nerve**.

## Final Word

*War of the Wizards* is at its best when you play it like the end of a long campaign. You have earned the Moonstone. You have allies in places you didn't have them in Book 1. You have power that finally matches the danger. Save enough of yourself to use all three when the wall of fire opens.

*Little time and much to do, Grey Star.* The Shianti said that to you at the start. They mean it more now.
