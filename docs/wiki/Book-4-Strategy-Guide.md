# Book 4 Strategy Guide: War of the Wizards

This is the finale. You have the Moonstone, Shasarak is wounded but still terrifying, and the whole Shadakine Empire is starting to crack.

*War of the Wizards* is not just a victory lap. It asks you to spend power carefully, keep moving when the plains turn against you, and choose the moment when Grey Star finally stops running and faces the Wytch-king.

## Quick Start

For your first clear, play like a survivor with a crown jewel in one hand.

Keep **Willpower** high. The Moonstone makes you stronger, but Book 4 is full of small magical costs, huge magical costs, and a few ugly moments where the book asks whether you can afford the spell you want.

Take practical supplies when the Masbate offer them. A **Sword**, **Coil of Rope**, **Tinderbox**, **Water Bottle**, and **Masbate Battle Horn** are not glamorous, but this book rewards the player who stays prepared.

Use the assistant for every combat with a preset. Book 4 loves timed fights: win within three rounds, survive one fly-by pass, or get pushed into a different section because the fight dragged on too long.

## What This Book Is Really Testing

Book 4 tests whether you can finish the job without burning yourself out.

You are powerful now. The Moonstone is real power, and the Higher Magicks give you more answers than you had in the earlier books. The trap is thinking every problem should be solved with magic.

The best run keeps two questions in mind:

- Can I solve this without spending Willpower?
- If I spend Willpower here, will I still have enough when Shasarak finally appears?

That is the heart of the book. You are not weak anymore, but you can still arrive at the final duel empty.

## Best First Playthrough

Listen to the Shianti at the start, then get moving.

On the Lissan Plain, do not wander blindly if you have a safer scouting option. Prophecy and Visionary paths can cost Willpower, but they often show you where the danger is before you step into it.

When you can prepare before the demon portal, prepare. The **Potion of Invulnerability** is one of the best tools in the book. If you can brew it or use it at the right time, it can turn a brutal demon stretch into something much more manageable.

Once the portal business is done, focus on the bigger war. The bridge, the Shadakine uniform, the Masbate, and the Freedom Guild all matter. Book 4 is about making the enemy army collide with the demon threat before you march into Shadaki for the final answer.

## Paths Worth Knowing

### Shianti Counsel

The opening choices are all useful, and they merge quickly.

Ask where you are being sent if you want practical geography. Ask for advice if you want the cleanest strategic nudge. Ask to see Shasarak if you want the mood of the finale right away.

None of these choices should lock you out of victory, so they are safe replay flavor.

### Lissan Plain And Phinomel Pods

The Lissan Plain is hot, empty, and mean.

If you find the Phinomel plant, the assistant can handle the roll and add the pods you gather. Those pods become much more interesting later if you reach the Leafwater pool in Fernmost. They can improve your Combat Skill with the Staff, which is a very nice thing to have before Shasarak.

For a clean first run, take the pods if the roll goes your way. For replay, come back and chase the better harvest.

### Masbate Rescue

The tortured Masbate survivor is one of the book's big turns.

This path can lead to traps, demon fights, and finally the reveal that Samu's people are not gone after all. It is worth seeing because it changes the whole shape of the war. You stop being two wanderers on the plain and become part of an army's last chance.

### Invulnerability Prep

If you can create or use a **Potion of Invulnerability**, do it thoughtfully.

The potion is strongest when the book is about to throw you into the demon press near Tilos. The assistant can remove the potion, return the empty vial, and mark the protection state so you do not have to remember it in the middle of a fight.

Thaumaturgy can also give you invulnerability protection, but it costs Willpower. The potion is often the sweeter deal.

### Demon Portal Of Tilos

This is one of the most dangerous sections of the book.

The Moonstone can push back the demon power, but the costs are real. Several portal choices use the special Book 4 Willpower rule: if you do not have enough Willpower, the book may let you take the hit as negative Willpower or use Endurance for the missing power.

Use the assistant's WP-cost buttons here. This is exactly the kind of bookkeeping the app is meant to keep clean.

### Shadakine Uniform

The uniform path is a clever one.

If you can take a Shadakine uniform, you can mislead the enemy and help aim the demon horde where it will do the most damage. It is not just a costume change. It is one of the cleanest ways Grey Star turns Shasarak's own war machine against him.

### Bridge At Lanzi

The bridge sequence is the big battlefield set-piece.

Expect expensive magic, hard combat, and timed results. The **Demon Master** fight is especially important: winning quickly sends you one way, while lasting too long sends you another. Let the Combat tab handle the round count.

### Freedom Guild And Fernmost

Fernmost is your last chance to breathe.

Rest when the book gives you rest. Take the healing. If you have Alchemy or Theurgy, the late herb cache is excellent. **Potion of Laumspur**, **Potion of Alether**, **Tarama Seeds**, **Temeris**, **Mustow**, and **Ezeran Acid** are all useful tools depending on the path you take.

If you gathered Phinomel Pods earlier, look for the Leafwater opportunity. A stronger Staff is exactly the sort of final-book reward you want.

### Shadaki And Shasarak

The final approach can come through teleportation, the Morn Pass, or direct challenge.

Once you reach Shasarak, read carefully. There are several ways the duel can unfold. You can fight him directly, use the Moonstone, or end up in a desperate test of Willpower and Endurance.

When the Staff shatters, the assistant removes it. That is not a bug. That is the story reaching its last page.

### Agarash

Do not let the finale trick you into thinking Shasarak is the only danger.

Agarash is the deeper threat behind the wall of fire. The ending is about stopping both the Wytch-king and the disaster waiting behind him. If the book offers you a final chance to close the portal, take the moment seriously.

## Items Worth Chasing

The **Potion of Invulnerability** is the standout. It can protect you through some awful demon fighting.

**Phinomel Pods** are easy to underestimate. They matter because of Leafwater.

The **Masbate Battle Horn** is a great story item, and it belongs in any replay where you are exploring the battlefield side of the book.

**Potion of Laumspur (+6 END)** is simple and excellent. Take it when you can.

**Potion of Alether** is a nice pre-fight boost, especially if you are heading toward the final stretch.

**Tarama Seeds** are powerful but easy to misuse. Treat each seed like one free spell or long-range Staff use, not like an infinite combat trick.

**Potion of Temeris** can save Willpower on teleportation choices. That can matter a lot near the end.

## Combat And Rolls

Book 4 has a lot of fights, and many of them are not ordinary.

Some fights care how many rounds you take. Some are one-round fly-by attacks. Some let you ignore your Endurance loss if you hurt the enemy more than it hurts you. Some have invulnerability variants. The assistant now has presets for the detected Book 4 fights, so start combat from the section controls whenever you can.

Use the roll helper too. Book 4 rolls often add current Endurance, current Willpower, both together, or bonuses from special items and Magicks. The roll button keeps the math tidy.

## Common Mistakes

The first mistake is treating the Moonstone like free power. It is not free.

The second mistake is ignoring Phinomel Pods because they look like a side item. They can become a Staff boost later.

The third mistake is forgetting that invulnerability is temporary. Use it when the danger is right in front of you.

The fourth mistake is letting timed combats run without tracking the round count. In Book 4, one extra round can change the section you go to.

The fifth mistake is reaching Shasarak with no Willpower left and hoping the finale will be gentle. It will not.

## Achievement Cleanup

First, finish the book. That should cover **Book Four Complete**, **Moonstone Bearer**, **Staff in Ashes**, **Agarash Defied**, and **Wizard Regent** on a clean final path.

Second, replay the Lissan Plain for **Phinomel Harvest** and **Blue-Light Brew**.

Third, replay the portal preparation and demon-front sections for **Untouchable For Now**, **Portal Breaker**, and **Bridge at Lanzi**.

Fourth, spend a run on the war story: **Masbate Found**, **Borrowed Colors**, **Freedom Guild Reached**, and **Fernmost Respite**.

Finally, clean up combat achievements with the Combat tab open. Book 4 has plenty of hard enemies, low-ratio fights, and long round counts for **War-Worn Wizard** and **Last Stand Nerve**.

## Final Word

*War of the Wizards* is at its best when you play it like the end of a long campaign.

You have earned the Moonstone. You have allies. You have power. Save enough of yourself to use all three when the wall of fire opens.
