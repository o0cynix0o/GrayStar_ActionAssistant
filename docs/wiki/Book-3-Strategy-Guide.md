# Book 3 Strategy Guide — *Beyond the Nightmare Gate*

> *"Strange shapes and pale faces writhe within the tunnel wall … 'Such a place could drive a man insane,' you say, quietly."*
>
> — Section 1

That's how Book 3 starts. You and Tanith, hand in hand, walking down a corridor lined with the staring faces of the lost. The Shadow Gate fades behind you. There's no going back the way you came. When the tunnel finally opens, you step out onto a *vast plain of white cloud suspended beneath a sunless sky* — no ground, no shadow, no direction. Welcome to the Daziarn.

*Beyond the Nightmare Gate* is the weird one, in the best possible way. You are out of Magnamund. The rules of reality are bending. The book keeps asking, again and again, whether Grey Star can stay steady when nothing around him makes sense.

A heads-up before we begin: this guide is spoiler-friendly for Books 1, 2, and 3 — characters, items, and decision points show up by name. It does not spoil what happens after the Moonstone returns to your hand. If you've already finished Book 2, you can read the whole thing without dimming the finale.

## Quick Start

For your first clear, play like a cautious pilot. Keep **Willpower** healthy, keep a **Length of Strong Rope** handy, and take the Ethetron seriously once you reach Section 116. The Assistant now handles all of Book 3's big bookkeeping pieces: Ethetron loot, key items, random rolls, combat presets, weapon confiscation, Backpack stashing, Senara healing, and the strange combat at the climax.

Your first goal is Section **350**. Do not worry about collecting every key, every side item, or every achievement on the first pass. Book 3 is dense enough that one focused run will give you plenty of story and still leave half the achievements waiting for a return trip.

## What This Book Is Really Testing

Book 1 tested whether you understood the cost of magic. Book 2 tested whether you could survive a long, punishing journey. Book 3 tests your **judgment** when the world stops behaving the way you expect it to.

The Daziarn is full of choices that feel sideways. Sometimes the brave-looking move is a trap. Sometimes losing a fight is the path forward. Sometimes the right answer is not to overpower a problem but to figure out what the book is actually asking you. The best Book 3 run is calm and deliberate. Let the Assistant carry the numbers so you can focus on the choice in front of you.

## Best First Playthrough

Take the practical road through the Crystal Tower and the Ethetron. When the book offers a choice between spending Willpower and trying a physical or item-based option, *think hard before spending*. Book 3 gives Willpower back in places, but it also drains it in little bites that add up faster than they look.

When you reach the flying-machine stretch around Section 116, keep your inventory clean. Take the **Gyronome** automatically, and seriously consider the **Length of Strong Rope** and **Potion of Healing** the section offers. The Rope helps with tower-climbing paths later, and the healing potion is exactly the sort of thing you'll be glad to see at the wrong end of a bad fight.

Once you're on the road to Section 350, don't panic at the final Jahksa encounter. The book has a very specific trick there, and the Assistant is built to follow it. (More on that in a minute.)

## Paths Worth Knowing

### Crystal Tower Keys

The Crystal Tower is the first real landmark you spot once you step onto the Daziarn plain, and the book does not undersell it:

> *A tall tower, gleaming faintly with a strange, brittle light. The tower appears to be constructed from great shards of black crystal.*
>
> — Section 211

Inside it lives one of the big replay toys in Book 3: the animal keys. For the cleanest first-run route, the **Serpent Key** is the answer you care about. The other keys are still worth exploring later — they lead to the Eagle, Wolf, Dragon, and Spider branches, each with their own fights, damage, and achievement targets.

If you're replaying, use the key route as a focused pass. Chase **Keybearer** and **Serpent Solution** first, then branch out into the animal-key scenes you skipped the first time.

### The Ethetron and the Singing City

Section 116 is the big handoff. This is where the book stops being a foot-travel adventure and becomes an airship voyage through impossible realms. And what an airship it is:

> *"It is the raw stuff of the Daziarn, unformed power for the craft," explains Crabkey. "Without it, the craft would fall from the sky."*
>
> — Section 116

So that's what you're flying. Take the Gyronome, pick your supplies, and use the roll helper whenever the Ethetron gets shaky. Several Ethetron rolls add your current Endurance, Willpower, Combat Skill, or a special item bonus, so the Assistant is much safer than mental math in the middle of a turbulence sequence.

Reaching the Singing City opens up a lot of the book's personality — and it's also where the Elessin can make your inventory life *interesting*.

### Elessin Gear Trouble

Book 3 has two different gear problems, and they are absolutely not the same:

- Sometimes the Elessin take your **weapons**. That should not wipe your Backpack.
- Later, you may leave your **Backpack** in the Ethetron while keeping your Staff. That should not erase your weapons.

The Assistant treats those as separate states. When the book gives things back, let the Assistant restore the right pile. Don't manually edit your inventory to "help" — you'll cross the streams and the recovery section will hand you the wrong stuff.

### Screaming God and the Guardian

The Screaming God path is one of the most memorable replay routes in the book. It has danger, lore, and a Guardian thread that lands harder than you'd expect. The important thing is stamina — several checks around the statue lean on Endurance, so don't wander into that path half-broken unless you're deliberately testing the danger. This is the cleanest pass for **Guardian's Song**.

### Chaos-Birds

The Chaos-bird stretch is messy, loud, and dangerous. Use the Combat tab for the bird fights. If Tanith is armed to help defend against them, make sure that status is marked so you remember the bonus option. If you use Elementalism, apply the Willpower cost before rolling. This stretch is a strong replay target for **Chaos-Bird Survivor**.

### The Paradox Route

The Paradox route is the *heart* of Book 3. It's strange, sad, and expensive. If you want to rescue Tanith cleanly, bring enough Willpower to stay with the chain — the Assistant tracks the repeated costs, but you still need to decide when the emotional choice is worth the price. Don't go in poor.

Replay this path for **Paradox Bargain** and **Tanith Remembered**.

### The Vale and Senara

The Vale is where the book lets you breathe — and just when you're starting to relax, it reminds you the antagonist is still watching:

> *"You have saved the people and all live happily ever after,"* he sneers, sarcastically. *"But other dangers await you."*
>
> — The Jahksa, Section 169

That's the Vale in a nutshell. Help comes with a cost, kindness draws attention, and the Jahksa is always just a section away. Pick up the Senara anyway. Senara buds and Senara potions restore Willpower, which makes them more valuable than they look at first glance. In a saga where Willpower is your real lifeline, an item that *gives it back* is gold. If you have the Herb Pouch space, take them. If you can make a potion, even better. The Assistant can add and use Senara items, so this is a real recovery stop rather than another bookkeeping chore.

### The Final Fight at Section 243

The fight at Section 243 is not a normal "win the fight, win the book" moment. Watch the text carefully when you get there:

> *A blinding clash of magical fire fills the room with light. "No, Grey Star," Tanith shouts. "Do not fight him."*
>
> — Section 243

Read that line twice. Tanith almost never gives you combat advice — when she does, she has a reason. You can stop fighting and follow the book's warning, or you can fight on to the strange ending that the situation creates. The Assistant supports the unusual result: combat defeat at Section 243 can carry you into Section 350 instead of a death screen.

That's a perfect example of Book 3's style. The book wants you to *understand* the moment, not to overpower it.

## Items Worth Chasing

The **Gyronome** matters. It is not flashy, but it is one of the book's main campaign items.

The **Length of Strong Rope** is a strong practical pick. Book 3 has enough climbing and awkward movement that it earns its place in the Backpack.

The **Potion of Healing** is simple and useful. Keep it visible.

The **Crystal Tower keys** are replay fuel. The Serpent Key is the clean answer; the others are worth seeing when you're achievement hunting.

**Senara Buds** and **Senara Potions** are excellent because they restore Willpower. In Grey Star, that is the rarest kind of magic.

The **Elessin loot** around Section 305 is interesting, but don't overload your Backpack just because the list is tempting. Take what supports the run you're actually making.

## Combat and Rolls

Use the Assistant for every Book 3 fight that has a preset. Book 3 has normal fights, survival fights, a door treated like an opponent, no-weapon Elessi fights, Chaos-bird fights, and the final encounter with its special ending behavior. That is too much to track casually.

Use the roll helper too. Several Book 3 rolls are not just 0-9 — they add Endurance, Willpower, Combat Skill, the Talisman, or the Silver Charm. The helper keeps the math tidy when the action is fast.

## Common Mistakes

The first mistake is spending Willpower like Book 3 is short. It is not.

The second mistake is confusing weapon confiscation with Backpack loss. If your weapons are taken, your Backpack should still be there. If your Backpack is left in the Ethetron, your Staff should still be there. The states are separate and the Assistant respects that — please don't "fix" the sheet manually.

The third mistake is ignoring the Serpent Key clue and brute-forcing the key route. That's a fun replay choice — not a smart first-clear choice.

The fourth mistake is treating the final fight like ordinary combat. Read that moment carefully. The book is telling you something.

## Achievement Cleanup

Handle Book 3 achievements in passes. First, finish the book — that should cover **Book Three Complete**, **Moonstone Claimed**, **Final Truth**, and the main route achievements. Second, replay the Crystal Tower key path for **Keybearer** and **Serpent Solution**, then sample the other key branches. Third, replay for the Elessin and inventory achievements: **Weapons Taken**, **Weapons Returned**, **Ethetron Stash**, and **Ethetron Recovery**. Fourth, replay the softer but important story paths: **Guardian's Song**, **Paradox Bargain**, **Tanith Remembered**, **Senara Brewer**. Finally, clean up combat achievements with the Combat tab open — Chaos-birds, the Daemonak, and the low-ratio victories are much easier when the app is tracking the fight.

## Final Word

*Beyond the Nightmare Gate* rewards patience more than aggression. For the first clear, conserve Willpower, respect the Ethetron, follow the Serpent Key clue, and let the final fight play out the way the book asks. For replay, pick one strange thread at a time and enjoy how *different* Book 3 feels from the first two.

The Daziarn is not Magnamund. The rules are not the same. That's the whole point — and it's also the whole pleasure.
