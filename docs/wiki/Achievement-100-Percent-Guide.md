# The Grey Star Action Assistant — 100% Achievement Guide

There's a moment, somewhere late in the fourth book, when the wall of fire splits open and Agarash the Damned drags himself back into the world. *"Free, free at last!"* he howls, and a vast flaming shoulder pushes out from the portal as Shasarak — broken, ruined Shasarak — vanishes screaming into the demon's old prison. You're standing there with the last of your Willpower and the last of your Endurance, and the game asks you one final question. Attack? Or close the portal?

That's the kind of moment the Grey Star series saves up for you. Four books of running, fighting, brewing, hiding, befriending, and bleeding — all of it pointed at a handful of decisions that *matter*. And if you're reading this guide, you don't just want to make those decisions. You want to make every decision, find every herb, win every fight, and walk away with all **102 achievements** lit up green in the Grey Star Action Assistant.

Good news: when we ran the full sweep ourselves, we came back at exactly that — **102 of 102**. Every badge, every flag, every gear-loss and gear-recovery, every wizard, every duel. It's all reproducible. The bad news, if you can call it that, is that it isn't reproducible *in one playthrough*. You can't do it. The achievements were designed against each other in a few key places, and a single straight-line run will always leave you stranded somewhere in the fifties out of a hundred and two.

So here's the deal. This is not a heroic single-quest guide. This is a **completionist sweep** — a deliberate, careful, two-pass plan that treats the books like a checklist instead of a story. You'll still get the story. You'll get more of it than most readers ever see. But you're going in with a plan, and the plan looks like this:

**Book 1 needs two runs. Books 2, 3, and 4 each need one extended sweep.** Five total runs to clear the whole series.

Let's talk through what each one looks like, and — more importantly — why.

## The Shape of the Sweep

When you finish all five runs, your tallies should land here:

|Book|Achievements|Notes|
|----|------------|-----|
|Book 1 — *Grey Star the Wizard*|31 / 31|The two-run book|
|Book 2 — *The Forbidden City*|25 / 25|Single broad sweep|
|Book 3 — *Beyond the Nightmare Gate*|23 / 23|Single broad sweep|
|Book 4 — *War of the Wizards*|23 / 23|Single broad sweep|
|**Total**|**102 / 102**|The whole saga|

A straight, story-focused playthrough — no sweeping, just playing — typically lands at about **51/102**. That's roughly half. If your first attempt comes back with a number like that, you're not playing badly. You're playing *normally*. The sweep is what gets you the other half, and it's a deliberate skill on top of the books themselves.

One more thing before we go in. The single most important strategic rule in this entire guide is about Book 1, and it'd be cruel to bury it past the next two thousand words: **on your first run through Book 1, do not raise the Sorcery shield before the Kleasa fight.** That's it. That's the rule. Hold that thought.

For the underlying route work that this sweep builds on, see the [Full Campaign Story Run](Full-Campaign-Story-Run).

## Book 1: *Grey Star the Wizard*

> *"Grey Star, the time has come for you to leave. Far will you travel and perilous is your course … Let not courage defeat wisdom."*
>
> — Maiteya, in the temple of Amida, Section 1

Every Grey Star achievement run starts the same way: a grim chill morning on the Isle of Lorn, an old teacher's hands on your shoulders, and a sail boat waiting on the shore. Your Shianti masters give you their silent blessing, Maiteya gives you that lovely warning about courage and wisdom, and you push off into the Sea of Dreams bound for the mainland of the Shadakine Empire. Don't get too comfortable — by the time you finish Book 1, you'll be a fugitive, a thief, and the survivor of an encounter with one of the nastiest single creatures in the entire saga.

But before we get to her, let's talk about why this book is the one that needs two runs.

### The Order Trap

Somewhere around the midpoint of Book 1 you reach a scene where Mother Magri's shadow comes for you out of a fire. At Section 139 the book gives you the option to spend two Willpower points and raise a Sorcery shield:

> *"At the cost of 2 WILLPOWER points, a shimmering field of energy encircles you."*
>
> — Section 139

This is the encounter with the Kleasa, and it's where the achievement design forces your hand. If you raise the shield, you survive the fight handily and earn **Shield Raised**. If you *don't* raise the shield, you take the hard way through, and surviving on the other side earns you **No Shield, No Problem**. The two are mutually exclusive in a single run — the shield either goes up or it doesn't — and the cleanup logic for this book absolutely insists that **No Shield, No Problem** is unlocked first.

Why this order? Two reasons. The practical one is that **No Shield, No Problem** is much harder to clean up on a backwards retry — a Grey Star who has already raised the shield once doesn't slip cleanly back into the no-shield version of the fight, and players who try this the other way around end up doing extra replays to recover the achievement. The story reason is that it's a better arc. You go through the worst version of the fight first — scarred, barely standing, half-dead by the end — and then you come back later, wiser, and do it the clean way. The hero learns. That's the rule.

So: **no-shield first, shield second.** Burn it in.

### Book 1, Run 1 — The No-Shield Finish

For this run, play Book 1 the way the author probably imagined you would — without the shield trick. Take the story route from the Full Campaign Story Run in the Assistant. The only thing you absolutely cannot do is pass through Section 139 *before* the Kleasa result fires, because that's the shield gate. Skip 139, take the hit, and survive on the other side.

What you should walk away with after this run is a substantial list of achievements — **29 out of 31** for Book 1. That's almost everything. You'll have your main story unlocks (**Book One Complete**, **The Long Road**, the **Lone Road**, **Final Ascent**), the centerpiece **No Shield, No Problem**, and a whole grab-bag of the side beats Book 1 wants you to hit: claiming and losing the **Kazim Stone**, picking up the **Priest's Amulet**, accepting **Jnana's Blessing**, the **Yabari Ward**, the **Alchemy Cache**, the **Prophetic Climb** up the mountain, the **Najin Standoff**, surviving the Kleasa, finding the **Correct Key**, the **Quoku Breakthrough**, losing and recovering your gear, watching **Tanith's Sacrifice**, and the slow climb of combat and survival milestones along the way.

A pro tip while you're in there: keep your Endurance up. The **Hard Finish** achievement only triggers if you cross the finish line still standing strong, and there's no late-game pickup that'll save you from limping in at single digits. Play smart on the back half, dodge fights you can dodge, and don't burn Willpower on flourishes when you could be saving it for the climb.

When you finish Run 1, the two achievements you'll be missing — and you'll be missing them on *purpose* — are **Leap of Faith** and **Shield Raised**. That's expected, and Run 2 is built around exactly those two.

### Book 1, Run 2 — The Shield + Side-Prize Replay

Here's where the Assistant earns its keep. Use the **Repeat Book** function instead of starting a new save. Repeat Book keeps your character intact and, crucially, keeps your achievement profile intact — every badge you earned in Run 1 stays earned. What resets is the current book attempt: your Endurance, your Willpower, and the route you're about to take.

The second run is short and surgical. You're not retreading the whole book; you're hitting a small set of beats:

- Raise the Sorcery shield this time. Walk straight into Section 139 and spend the two Willpower.
- Survive the Kleasa result *with* the shield up. **Shield Raised** lights up.
- Mix **Ezeran Acid** somewhere along the way — the alchemy side trip is easy to skip and easy to miss.
- Take the **Redeemer item route**.
- Make the **ravine jump**. This is **Leap of Faith** — don't chicken out and take the safe path around.
- Push through **Willpower Burnout** if it hasn't already triggered.

The Run 2 route we used hits exactly ten sections:

```text
1 → 139 → 149 → 165 → 297 → 209 → 49 → 274 → 4 → 350
```

That's it. Ten section taps and Book 1 closes out at **31/31**. Two runs, one perfect book, and now we leave the Isle of Lorn and the Kleasa behind us and head south into the empire proper.

## Book 2: *The Forbidden City*

Book 2 opens with you stranded high in the canopy of the great Azawood tree, in the wooden houses of the Kundi, the Lost Tribe of Lara — the people you found at the very end of Book 1, after the ape-like Kundi men dropped from the branches in a cloud of smoking leaves and beat back the Mantiz attack. King Okosa's first impression of you was, generously, *bad*. The Kundi shaman Urik tested you with a children's riddle:

> *"Wise Shianti and Kundi man,*
> *Look eye to eye in tree,*
> *Shianti man see Kundi man,*
> *But what does Kundi see?"*
>
> — Urik the Shaman, Book 1, Section 350

Book 2 is the answer. By the end of it, you'll have crossed half a continent, made unlikely allies with everyone from a swamp-dwelling giant to the Karnali slaves, brewed potions, broken chains, lost your gear (again), survived a bareback escape, crossed a bridge that almost certainly should not have held, faced a Scree Wyrm, and finally opened the Shadow Gate into a place the books call the Daziarn — a kind of nightmare-mirror to your own world. It's a *lot*, and it's why Book 2 is the longest sweep in the saga.

But here's the lovely thing: **Book 2 doesn't trap you the way Book 1 does.** There's no mutually exclusive achievement pair, no order rule, no "do this before that." Book 2 just wants you to *slow down* and visit everywhere it has to show you. So pick your normal Book 2 story route and treat it as a baseline, then build a side-quest layer on top.

The side-quest layer is dense, so think of it less as a checklist and more as a sightseeing list. You'll want to fight or at least record the **Swamp Giant** encounter and the **Magdi** path — these are both big, distinctive route branches that often get skipped on a story run because they look optional. They're optional in the sense that the book lets you skip them, but the achievements absolutely notice. Meet **Chaksu** along the way. Ally with **Sado**. Take **Samu's oath** route. Help **Karnali**, and follow through on **breaking the slave route** — these are connected emotionally to the same liberation arc, so you'll want to do them as a piece. Stop at the **Forbidden City** itself; reach the **Shadow Gate**; and crucially, open it.

On the item front, Book 2 has a generous spread of pickups and they're scattered widely enough that a careless run will miss two or three. Collect the **Silver Knife**, brew or pick up the **Karmo Potion**, claim the **Black Rod**, the **Mind Gem**, and gather **Azawood** while you're up in the canopy. Hit the **Deathgaunt ward** route — it's its own little narrative beat that's easy to bypass.

And then there's the back-half checklist: lose and recover your **gear** somewhere in the middle (the book has a couple of capture-and-escape sequences that handle this naturally), take the **bareback escape** during one of them, survive the **bridge crossing**, face the **Scree Wyrm**.

If you cover all of that in one run — and you can; it just takes patience — Book 2 closes out at a clean **25/25**.

## Book 3: *Beyond the Nightmare Gate*

Book 3 is the strangest of the four, and probably the most beautiful. You step through the Shadow Gate at the end of Book 2 and find yourself in the Daziarn, a realm where reality bends in ways your Shianti training didn't fully prepare you for. There's a **Crystal Tower**. There's a **Singing City**. There's a flying machine called the **Ethetron** that you're going to pilot, badly, before you figure it out. And there's Tanith — yes, that Tanith, the one whose sacrifice you watched at the end of Book 1 — alive in some sense, here in this place, waiting to be rescued.

Book 3 is also where you finally get your hands on the **Moonstone of the Shianti**, the artifact your entire quest has been about from the beginning. The book's ending, when the Jahksa makes its killing blow and the Moonstone turns the strike back on the attacker, is one of the saga's quiet show-stopping moments:

> *"You take up the Moonstone. It flares, as if in recognition, at the touch of your hand … Tanith's eyes are wild and shining with triumph."*
>
> — Section 350

You want to walk away from Book 3 with that stone in your hand. Book 4 cannot start without it.

Like Book 2, the cleanup pass here is one extended run rather than two. Use your normal Book 3 finish route as the spine and layer the side targets on top. Defeat or record the **Daemonak** route. **Pilot the Ethetron** — and don't skip the moment where you have to **stash and recover** its supplies, because that's its own achievement. Reach the **Singing City**. Open the **Crystal Tower** and become a **Keybearer**. Solve the **serpent route** — it's the puzzle gate that wants the keys you just collected. Hear the **Guardian's Song**. Lose and recover your weapons (a running theme in this saga — Grey Star spends a remarkable amount of his career being disarmed and re-armed). Survive the **Chaos-Bird**. Take the **paradox bargain** when it's offered, even though every instinct in your body will tell you not to. Rescue **Tanith**. Meet the **Shadow Brother**. Reach the **Final Truth**. And finally, walk out the other side of the Daziarn with the **Moonstone** in your inventory.

One run, **23/23**, and you carry the Moonstone forward.

## Book 4: *War of the Wizards*

> *"As Shasarak disappears through the wall, Agarash calls out with glee, 'Free, free at last!' You glimpse a wing and a clawed foot."*
>
> — Section 350

That's where Book 4 takes you. The empire has collapsed into open war, the Wytch-king Shasarak is finally cornered, and his last gambit is to release something *worse* than himself rather than be defeated. Your job is to stop both of them with the last of your Willpower and the last of your Endurance and an artifact that's already cracked from earlier abuse.

Book 4 is a victory lap, but it's an *expensive* one. The book asks you to spend magic like you've got pockets full of it — brew the big potions, swing the big spells, and burn Willpower on flourishes you'd never have justified back in Book 1. Lean into it. You're not saving anything for tomorrow.

Start with the Moonstone — you brought it, right? — and run the standard Book 4 finish route while making sure you hit the cleanup beats. Gather **Phinomel Pods** when the early game gives you the chance; you'll need them for the centerpiece alchemy moment, which is to **brew the Potion of Invulnerability** and then actually **use it** in combat. Both are separate achievements, and a surprising number of players brew the potion and then hoard it in case they need it later. They never use it. They die holding it. Don't be that hero.

Find **Masbate** when the route offers him. **Close the Demon portal** when you reach the early portal scene — this is *not* the final portal at the climax; this is an earlier one and it's easy to walk past. **Take a Shadakine uniform** when the disguise option appears. Cross the bridge at **Lanzi**. Reach the **Freedom Guild**. Rest at **Fernmost** — yes, the rest itself is the achievement, so actually rest, don't push through. Collect the final **alchemy cache**. Use **Leafwater with the Staff** at the moment the book asks for it; this is one of the late-book combinations that's easy to skip because the choice is presented quietly.

The endgame is a sequence of named confrontations: reach **Shadaki**, face the **Ipage guardian**, duel **Shasarak**. When the Staff shatters in your hand — and it will shatter — let it. That moment is supposed to happen. The achievement reads **Staff Shattered**, which sounds like a failure state and is actually a story beat. Then **defy Agarash** at the climax, and complete the book in the title role: **Wizard Regent**.

One run, **23/23**, and the credits theme is yours.

## Keeping the Cleanup From Getting Messy

A few hard-won lessons before you head out the door.

**Use Repeat Book.** This is the single most important Assistant feature for completionist work. When you're cleaning up a book, Repeat Book keeps your character and your achievement profile intact and resets only the current book attempt, so you can try a different route without losing everything you've earned. Starting a brand-new save throws away exactly the progress you need to preserve. The whole guide is built around this.

**Don't sweat a low first-run number.** A clean, story-first playthrough lands around 51 of 102 — barely half. That's normal. You are not playing badly. You're playing the books the way they were written, and the books were written for readers, not for completionists. The other half is what the sweep is for.

**Combat achievements live in the Combat tab.** The Assistant records your combat history, and the achievements read from that history. If a fight isn't logged — if you resolved it longhand off-app, for instance — the achievement doesn't fire. Anything combat-flavored, run it through the Combat tab.

**Item achievements need real evidence.** The Assistant will backfill achievements from durable save history where it can, but it still needs proof you actually grabbed the item or visited the section where the proof lives. Saying you took the Silver Knife doesn't count. Taking it does.

**For Book 1: no-shield first, shield second.** One more time, in case you skimmed: the entire saga's completionist viability hinges on doing the Kleasa fight without the Sorcery shield on your first run, and *then* coming back for the shield route on Run 2. Inversely, Books 2, 3, and 4 don't care what order you sweep them in — they only care that you sweep them at all.

That's the whole plan. Five runs, two passes through Book 1 and one through each of the others, and somewhere on the far side of the wall of fire — past Shasarak's bargain and Agarash's clawed foot — you'll see the Assistant tick over to **102 / 102** and stay there.

Good luck out there, Wizard Regent. Don't forget the Moonstone.
