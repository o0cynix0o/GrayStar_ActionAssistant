# Book 2 Strategy Guide: The Forbidden City

This guide is a spoiler-friendly strategy article for **Book 2: The Forbidden City**. It uses the completed Book 2 audit, playtest report, achievements, and assistant support data to explain how the book works as a playable route system.

Book 2 is fully supported by the assistant, but its route-family names are still a lighter pass than Book 1's named route audit. The guide below keeps the big route shapes readable without pretending every branch has been given a final story label.

## Quick Answer

For a first clear, protect Willpower, keep route items visible, and let the assistant handle gear capture, gear return, Backpack discard, combat presets, and random-number helpers.

The successful route eventually reaches section `310`, but Book 2 is not only about the final section. It is about surviving a long chain of hostile places: swamp, captivity, resistance routes, the dead lands, the Forbidden City, and the final Shadow Gate sequence.

Do not try to collect every achievement in one run. Book 2 has strong replay targets: Chaksu Pipes, Silver Knife, Karmo Potion, Black Rod, Mind Gem, Karnali liberation, Samu, Azawood, Deathgaunt protection, Bareback Escape, and Shadow Gate completion.

## Story Summary

*The Forbidden City* takes the survival pressure of Book 1 and turns it into a harsher expedition. Grey Star is no longer simply trying to reach the next stage of the quest. He is moving through occupied lands, strange allies, broken cities, and places where magic is as likely to exhaust him as save him.

The tone is more oppressive than Book 1. The route repeatedly strips away comfort: gear can be taken, the Backpack can be lost, and the safest-looking choice is not always the one that preserves the most strength. The book wants the player to feel that every step toward the Forbidden City has a price.

It is also a better campaign book than it first appears. Items like the Black Rod and Mind Gem feel important because they belong to the larger story, not just the current fight. The best runs through Book 2 feel like you are building momentum toward the Shadow Gate, not simply surviving isolated encounters.

## How Book 2 Really Works

The Book 2 route graph is healthier than Book 1's raw source graph: all 310 sections are reachable from section `1`, and the local audit found no bad source links or no-incoming section oddities. That makes it a better book for route testing, but not an easier book to play.

The difficulty comes from layered resource pressure. Book 2 asks for Willpower, Endurance, Meals, route items, temporary gear loss, permanent Backpack loss, and combat survival. The assistant supports all of the audited mechanics, but you still need to make the strategic decision about which risks are worth taking.

The major difference from Book 1 is gear handling. Book 2 has temporary Staff/Backpack capture and later return, but it also has a permanent Backpack discard route. Those are not the same event. The assistant treats them differently, and so should you.

## The Best First Playthrough

For a first win, aim for stability. Do not treat every side route as mandatory, and do not spend Willpower just because a Magick option looks impressive.

The strongest first-run pattern is to keep moving toward the success spine while accepting only the side goals that clearly improve your odds or align with your current route. A route item is valuable when you can keep it, use it, or convert it into a meaningful later advantage. It is less valuable if collecting it leaves Grey Star too weak for the next forced test.

Use the assistant aggressively in this book. Book 2 has 130 supported mechanical-effect sections, 42 flow sections, 19 combat presets, 90 roll outcomes, loot buttons, and Willpower-cost buttons. That is a lot of bookkeeping. The app is there so you can think about the adventure instead.

## The Major Route Families

Book 2's route families are broad bands rather than tidy isolated paths. They overlap heavily, but each one has a different strategic identity.

### Swamp And Chaksu Route

The swamp route is the early test of whether you can manage attrition. It includes Meal checks, poisonous terrain, random outcomes, and several places where Magick can open or soften a path.

The Chaksu side of this family is one of the more satisfying replay targets because it has a clear discovery identity. **Chaksu Friend** and the Chaksu Pipes give the route a concrete payoff beyond simply surviving another branch.

For a first run, move through this part carefully. For replay, it is worth leaning into the Chaksu thread and related item pickups to see how much more texture the early book has.

### Karnali And Freedom Guild Route

The Karnali and resistance route family is where Book 2 starts feeling less like wilderness survival and more like a story about rebellion under occupation.

This family supports achievements such as **Karnali Liberator**, **Sado's Ally**, **Slave Breaker**, and **Samu's Oath**. Those are not just route stamps. They mark the book's shift into allies, plans, and consequences.

On a first clear, do not overextend just to chase every story beat. On replay, this is one of the best families to follow deliberately because it gives the book a stronger sense of place.

### Dead Lands, Samu, Hugi, And Deathgaunt Route

The dead lands route is where Willpower discipline starts to matter even more. Several sections apply direct WP loss, and the Deathgaunt-related routes test whether you have the right protection, item state, or magical route support.

Azawood and Evocation matter here. The assistant records Azawood uses and Deathgaunt protection routes where the audit confirmed them. This family is tied to **Azawood Gatherer** and **Deathgaunt Ward**, and it can also overlap with Samu-related achievements.

The mistake to avoid is treating protection items as decorative. In Book 2, a small protective route can be the difference between a controlled run and a collapse.

### Forbidden City Route

The Forbidden City family is the middle-to-late structure that gives the book its title. It is less about one single item and more about getting through a dense hostile route while keeping the campaign objective in sight.

The **Black Rod** and **Mind Gem** are the headline discoveries here. They are strong replay targets because they feel like proper story artifacts, and the assistant can track them as items or route evidence.

This is also where gear state becomes especially important. If the Staff and Backpack are taken, let the assistant store the unavailable gear. If they are returned, let the return section restore them. Manual inventory patching can confuse your own sense of what the book has actually done.

### Final Shadow Gate Route

The final route family runs into the Shadow Gate completion and section `310`. This is the Book 2 equivalent of a campaign handoff.

By the time you reach this point, your inventory and Willpower history matter. The Black Rod, Mind Gem, combat record, route achievements, and gear events all feed into the sense that this was a campaign chapter rather than a standalone maze.

The final completion achievement is **Shadow Gate Opened**. Treat it as the first-run priority. Once that is secure, repeat the book for cleaner item and story-route cleanup.

## Item And Discovery Routes That Matter

The most important Book 2 discoveries are the **Black Rod**, **Mind Gem**, **Chaksu Pipes**, **Silver Knife**, **Karmo Potion**, **Azawood Leaves**, and **Potion of Laumspur**.

The Black Rod and Mind Gem matter because they feel central to the campaign arc. They are not just useful labels in the inventory. They are story objects, and the achievements tied to them make them natural replay goals.

The Silver Knife and Chaksu Pipes are cleaner side-route rewards. They are easy to understand, easy to verify in the Inventory tab, and good examples of why a replay route can be satisfying even when it is not the shortest path to completion.

The Karmo Potion remains partly manual. The audit confirmed that the potion exists and the assistant can support the roll, but the book gives the player timing discretion about when to apply the Endurance penalty. That timing choice belongs to the player.

Azawood deserves special attention because it behaves like protection, not treasure. If you gather it, watch the sections where it can be used. The assistant supports the confirmed uses, but you still need to notice when the route is asking for it.

## Gear Loss, Capture, And Backpack Discard

Book 2 has two different gear problems.

The first is temporary capture. Section `149` stores unavailable Staff and Backpack gear, and section `200` restores that stored gear. This is a temporary loss and return. Let the assistant handle it.

The second is permanent Backpack discard. Section `297` discards Backpack contents and marks the Backpack unavailable. This is not the same as capture. Do not expect a later recovery section to rebuild a Backpack that the book forced you to abandon.

This distinction matters for both play and achievements. **Taken Captive** and **Gear Returned** belong to the capture/return arc. **Bareback Escape** belongs to the harsher Backpack-loss route.

## Combat And Random-Number Advice

Book 2 has enough special combat and random behavior that manual tracking becomes tedious quickly. The playtest report confirmed combat presets, roll helpers, and special cases such as the section `99` first-round exception.

Use the Combat tab for fights. Use the roll helper for random-number sections. Do not try to remember every modifier while also managing Willpower and inventory.

Combat achievements such as **First Blood Repaid**, **Second Trial, Sharp Edge**, **Dead Lands Veteran**, **Swamp Giant Felled**, **Magdi Breaker**, and **Scree Wyrm Slayer** are best cleaned up deliberately. A first completion run may unlock some of them, but a replay can target the missing fights more cleanly.

## Trap Choices And Common Mistakes

The first mistake is confusing "reachable" with "safe." The route graph says every section is reachable, but that does not mean every branch is a good first-run choice.

The second mistake is spending Willpower too freely in the first half of the book. Book 2 keeps finding new reasons to drain it. A Magick route that looks elegant early can become a problem later if you never stop spending.

The third mistake is mishandling gear. Temporary capture should be restored by the assistant. Permanent Backpack discard should not. If you treat both as the same event, your inventory will stop telling the truth.

The fourth mistake is forcing Karmo Potion handling too mechanically. Because the timing has player discretion, record the potion, use the assistant's support, and make the timing decision consciously.

## Achievement Cleanup

Book 2 cleanup is best split by route family.

Start with the completion run: reach section `310`, unlock **Book Two Complete**, and get **Shadow Gate Opened** if the completion route supports it. Along the way, you will probably unlock some combat, story, and item achievements naturally.

Then run a discovery pass for **Chaksu Friend**, **Silver Knife**, **Karmo Brewer**, **Black Rod Claimed**, **Mind Gem**, and **Azawood Gatherer**. This is the run where you slow down and let the item routes matter.

Finally, run a survival and combat pass for **Deathgaunt Ward**, **Bareback Escape**, **Bridge Survivor**, **Scree Wyrm Slayer**, **Swamp Giant Felled**, **Magdi Breaker**, and any remaining combat-round achievements. Repeat Book is useful here because you can keep the same character and achievements while resetting the book state.

## Final Recommendation

Book 2 rewards patience more than boldness. It is bigger, harsher, and more mechanically layered than Book 1, but the route graph is stable and the assistant support is strong.

For a first clear, keep Willpower in reserve, respect gear-state changes, and focus on reaching the Shadow Gate. For replay, choose one route family at a time and let the achievements guide the cleanup. That is where *The Forbidden City* becomes more than a difficult second book. It becomes a campaign chapter you can actually master.
