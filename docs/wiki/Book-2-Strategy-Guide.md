# Book 2 Strategy Guide: The Forbidden City

Book 2 is where Grey Star's journey gets meaner. The road is longer, the land is harsher, and the book is much more willing to take things away from you.

This guide is here to help you plan a good run through *The Forbidden City* without turning the adventure into a spreadsheet. Use it for first-clear advice, replay goals, item priorities, and the big danger spots to watch.

## Quick Start

For your first clear, play patiently.

Keep **Willpower** in reserve, keep important items visible in your inventory, and let the assistant handle capture, gear return, Backpack loss, random-number rolls, and combat presets.

Your goal is to reach section `310` and open the way forward. Everything else is a bonus until you have that first win.

## What This Book Is Really Testing

Book 1 teaches you that magic has a cost. Book 2 asks whether you actually learned that lesson.

The danger here is not just one bad fight. It is the long grind: swamp travel, hostile soldiers, strange allies, gear loss, deadly terrain, and the final push toward the Shadow Gate. A choice that looks fine in the moment can hurt badly ten sections later if it drains too much Willpower or leaves your Backpack in a mess.

The best first run is not the boldest run. It is the one where you keep Grey Star steady enough to survive the whole chain.

## Best First Playthrough

Move with purpose. Take useful help, but do not chase every side reward just because it is there.

Book 2 has a lot of moving parts: Meals, Willpower costs, Endurance hits, combat, route items, temporary gear capture, and permanent Backpack loss. The assistant can handle the bookkeeping, but you still need to decide whether a risk is worth taking.

When in doubt, preserve Willpower. If Book 2 offers you a magical shortcut, ask yourself whether you will still be comfortable two fights later.

## Paths Worth Knowing

These are the big paths to think about when planning a run.

### Swamp And Chaksu Route

The swamp is the early endurance test. It is full of small losses, route checks, random results, and moments where the wrong choice can make the rest of the book feel heavier.

The Chaksu thread is one of the better replay targets. If you are going after **Chaksu Friend** or the Chaksu Pipes, slow down and play this part deliberately instead of rushing through it.

For a first clear, take the practical road. For replay, let the swamp breathe a little and chase the side discoveries.

### Karnali And The Freedom Guild

This is where Book 2 starts feeling like a resistance story. You are not just crossing bad country anymore. You are meeting people who are trying to fight back.

Achievements like **Karnali Liberator**, **Sado's Ally**, **Slave Breaker**, and **Samu's Oath** live in this part of the adventure. They are great replay goals because they make the world feel more alive.

On a first run, help where it makes sense, but do not wreck your character trying to force every heroic moment into one trip.

### Dead Lands And Deathgaunts

The dead lands are where carelessness starts to hurt. Willpower losses pile up, and protection items become more than nice little extras.

Azawood is the big one to respect. If you find it, keep track of it. It can matter when the book starts throwing Deathgaunt trouble at you.

This is a good path for a focused survival replay. If you want **Azawood Gatherer** or **Deathgaunt Ward**, plan around this stretch instead of hoping it happens by accident.

### Forbidden City Route

The Forbidden City portion is the heart of the book. It is dangerous, crowded, and full of story items that feel like they belong to the bigger campaign.

The **Black Rod** and **Mind Gem** are the headline prizes. They are worth chasing on replay because they feel important, not just useful. If you pick them up, make sure they are recorded properly so you can see them when the story calls back to them.

This is also where gear trouble becomes serious. If the book takes the Staff or Backpack, do not panic. Let the assistant store what is unavailable and restore it when the book gives it back.

### Shadow Gate Route

The final route pushes toward section `310`. By then, your earlier choices have shaped the run: what you carried, what you spent, who you helped, and how much strength Grey Star has left.

Do not limp into the ending if you can avoid it. Save Willpower, keep the Black Rod and Mind Gem straight if you have them, and use the assistant's completion flow when the book wraps.

## Items Worth Chasing

The **Black Rod** and **Mind Gem** are the big campaign-feeling items. If you are doing a serious replay, they deserve attention.

The **Chaksu Pipes** and **Silver Knife** are cleaner side-route treasures. They are good choices when you want a run that feels different without turning the whole book upside down.

The **Karmo Potion** is useful, but it needs a little player judgment. The book gives you some timing discretion around the penalty, so record the potion and make the timing choice carefully.

The **Azawood Leaves** are not treasure. They are protection. Treat them like insurance, because that is exactly how they feel when the dangerous routes arrive.

The **Potion of Laumspur** is the kind of item that is easy to forget until you need it. Keep healing items visible.

## Gear Capture And Backpack Loss

Book 2 has two different kinds of gear trouble, and mixing them up is the easiest way to confuse your inventory.

Sometimes your Staff and Backpack are taken temporarily. In that case, the assistant stores the gear and can return it when the recovery section arrives.

Other times, the book forces you to abandon Backpack contents. That is different. If the Backpack is truly discarded, do not expect it to come back later like captured gear.

The short version: temporary capture is a pause. Backpack discard is a loss.

## Combat And Rolls

Use the Combat tab. Book 2 has enough special fights that tracking them by hand is asking for trouble.

Use the roll helper too. A random-number section is much easier when the app shows the possible results right next to the roll.

If you are chasing combat achievements like **Swamp Giant Felled**, **Magdi Breaker**, or **Scree Wyrm Slayer**, do that on a replay. A first clear is about finishing the book, not picking every fight on the map.

## Common Mistakes

The first mistake is spending Willpower early because the choice looks exciting. Book 2 keeps asking for more. Save some.

The second mistake is treating every side route like it belongs in your first win. It does not. A focused run is stronger than a cluttered one.

The third mistake is confusing captured gear with discarded gear. If the assistant says your gear is stored, wait for the return. If the book says your Backpack is gone, believe it.

The fourth mistake is forgetting healing and protection items. Book 2 punishes messy inventory habits.

## Achievement Cleanup

Handle achievements in passes.

First, complete the book and open the Shadow Gate. Get the win on the board.

Second, do an item run. Aim for Chaksu Pipes, Silver Knife, Karmo Potion, Black Rod, Mind Gem, and Azawood Leaves.

Third, do a story and combat run. Chase Karnali, Sado, Samu, Deathgaunt protection, Bareback Escape, Bridge Survivor, Swamp Giant, Magdi, and Scree Wyrm achievements.

Trying to force all of that into one run will make the book feel messy. Give each pass a job.

## Final Word

*The Forbidden City* rewards patience. It is bigger and harsher than Book 1, but it is fairer when you let each run have a purpose.

For the first clear, protect Willpower and get to `310`. For replay, pick the path you want and enjoy the hunt. That is where Book 2 starts to feel less like survival and more like mastery.
