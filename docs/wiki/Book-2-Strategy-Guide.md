# Book 2 Strategy Guide — *The Forbidden City*

> *"Black mountains! And beyond … a wide valley, dead land, ash and dust … the Shadow Gate — beneath the light of a full moon."*
>
> — Urik the Shaman, Section 1

Welcome to the road to the Shadow Gate. Book 1 was a road story too, but Book 2 is the version of that story where the road has *teeth*. You start it half-dead in a hammock in the canopy of the great Azawood tree, healed by three days of "Kundi magic" you didn't ask for and don't entirely understand. Old Urik, the comical bow-legged Shaman with the bells and the toothless grin, has just had his vision. Black mountains. Dead land. A full moon. Two weeks to cross two hundred miles of Shadakine country before the gate opens and closes again.

That's your assignment. *The Forbidden City* is the journey there.

A heads-up before we begin: this guide is spoiler-friendly for Book 1 and Book 2 — characters, items, and decision points show up by name. It does not spoil anything from what lies beyond the Shadow Gate. If you've already finished Book 1, you can read the whole thing without dimming the next leg of the journey.

## Quick Start

For your first clear, play **patiently**. Keep **Willpower** in reserve, keep important items visible in your inventory, and let the Assistant handle the bookkeeping that Book 2 throws at you constantly — capture and gear return, Backpack loss, random-number rolls, combat presets. Your goal is to reach Section **310** and open the way forward. Everything else is bonus until you have that first win on the board.

If Book 1 made you cautious, good. Book 2 will reward that. If Book 1 made you reckless because you got away with it, *Book 2 will fix that for you*.

## What This Book Is Really Testing

Book 1 taught you that magic has a cost. Book 2 asks whether you actually learned the lesson. The danger here is not one bad fight — it's the long grind. Swamp travel. Hostile soldiers. Strange allies who may or may not be trying to help you. Deadly terrain that doesn't even need a monster to kill you. And underneath all of it, a count-down: Urik's vision said two weeks, and the book means it.

A choice that looks fine in the moment can hurt badly ten sections later if it drains too much Willpower or leaves your Backpack in a mess. The best first run is not the boldest. It's the one where you keep Grey Star steady enough to survive the whole chain from the Azawood treetops to the dead land beyond the Mountains of Morn.

## Best First Playthrough

Move with purpose. Take useful help when it's offered. Don't chase every side reward just because it's there. Book 2 has a *lot* of moving parts — Meals, Willpower costs, Endurance hits, route items, temporary gear capture, permanent Backpack loss — and the Assistant can carry the bookkeeping, but it can't decide for you whether a risk is worth it.

Urik, for what it's worth, has thoughts on the matter. When you ask him about food and supplies before you leave the cloud forest, he's blunt:

> *"Food grows on the land … water falls from sky … only foolish man wish to carry the earth and the sky upon his back in little bag."*
>
> — Urik, Section 126

That's basically the whole Book 2 philosophy in one quote. Travel light. Take what you need. Don't waste energy on things the land will give you anyway. The honest rule of thumb: when in doubt, preserve Willpower. If Book 2 offers you a magical shortcut, ask yourself whether you'll still be comfortable two fights from now. If the answer is "probably not," take the long way.

## Paths Worth Knowing

These are the threads worth thinking about before you set out. You won't see all of them on a single run, and you shouldn't try to.

### Swamp and Chaksu Route

The swamp is the early endurance test. Small losses, route checks, random results, and a steady drumbeat of "the wrong choice here makes the rest of the book heavier." The Chaksu thread runs through this stretch — Chaksu himself is a quiet, sympathetic presence in a country that's mostly trying to kill you, and the achievement chain around him (**Chaksu Friend**, the Chaksu Pipes) is one of the more rewarding replay targets.

For a first clear, take the practical road through the swamp. For replay, slow down. Let the swamp breathe. The discoveries are worth the time.

### Karnali and the Freedom Guild

This is where Book 2 stops being a wilderness travelogue and starts feeling like a resistance story. You're not just crossing bad country anymore. You're meeting people who are organizing to fight back. Karnali is their city. Sado of the Long Knife is the man with the plan.

Sado is *also* the man who will absolutely use you for his own purposes if you let him — and even if you don't:

> *A small escort leads you through the city streets. Suddenly, you notice you are passing the stone fortress of the Shadakine — you have been duped by Sado …*
>
> — Section 287

That's the flavor of the Karnali stretch in one snapshot. The resistance is *desperate*, the leaders are willing to play rough, and you can easily end up steering a riot you didn't sign up for. Achievements like **Karnali Liberator**, **Sado's Ally**, **Slave Breaker**, and **Samu's Oath** all live in this part of the adventure. They're great replay goals because they make the world feel *alive* — the Shadakine Empire stops being scenery and starts being a thing that real people are pushing against. On a first run, help where it makes sense, but don't wreck your character trying to force every heroic moment into one trip.

### Dead Lands and Deathgaunts

The dead lands are where carelessness starts to *hurt*. Willpower losses pile up, the terrain itself is hostile, and protection items become much more than nice little extras. Some of the danger isn't even magical — it's just the kind of thing that happens when you walk into the wrong building in the wrong city:

> *Urik shouts a warning, but it is too late. A Dead City wretch coshes you with a wooden club. You do not have time to react and the blow knocks you unconscious.*
>
> — Section 166

A wooden club. After three books of demons and shadow-creatures, that line still lands. Book 2 will hurt you with mundane things if you let your guard down. Azawood is the big one to *respect* here. If you find it, keep track of it. It can matter when the book starts throwing Deathgaunt trouble at you, and the Deathgaunts do not negotiate.

This is a good stretch for a focused survival replay. If you want **Azawood Gatherer** or **Deathgaunt Ward**, plan around this section instead of hoping you happen across them.

### Forbidden City Route

The Forbidden City is the heart of the book — and yes, the book *is* named after it. Expect it to be dangerous, crowded, and full of campaign-feeling items. The city's defenses aren't all swords and soldiers, either; some are much older:

> *This wall of fire is nothing but an illusion, cast long ago by the wizard of Taklakot. You pass through the flames and they simply melt away.*
>
> — Section 240

That moment is the city in miniature. The obstacles look terrifying. Some of them aren't real. Others *very much* are, and the difference between them is your magical training. Trust what the Assistant tells you about magical detection — if a Magick says the wall isn't real, walk through it.

The **Black Rod** and the **Mind Gem** are the headline prizes here. They feel important because they are. If you pick them up, make sure they're recorded properly so you can see them when the story calls back to them. This is also where gear trouble gets serious. If the book takes the Staff or the Backpack, don't panic. Let the Assistant store what's unavailable and restore it when the book hands it back.

### Shadow Gate Route

The final route pushes toward Section 310. By the time you get there, your earlier choices have shaped the run: what you carried, what you spent, who you helped, how much strength Grey Star has left. Do not limp into the ending if you can avoid it. Save Willpower, keep the Black Rod and Mind Gem straight if you have them, and use the Assistant's completion flow when the book wraps.

Remember Urik's vision — *the Shadow Gate, beneath the light of a full moon*. You're racing a clock the whole book. Walk into 310 with something in the tank.

## Items Worth Chasing

The **Black Rod** and **Mind Gem** are the big campaign-feeling items. If you're doing a serious replay, they deserve attention.

The **Chaksu Pipes** and **Silver Knife** are cleaner side-route treasures. They're good choices when you want a run that feels different without turning the whole book upside down.

The **Karmo Potion** is useful, but it needs a little player judgment — the book gives you some timing discretion around the penalty, so record the potion and make the timing choice carefully.

**Azawood Leaves** are not treasure. They're *insurance*. Treat them accordingly, because that's exactly how they feel when the dangerous routes arrive.

The **Potion of Laumspur** is the kind of item that is easy to forget until the moment you desperately need it. Keep your healing items visible in the inventory.

## Gear Capture and Backpack Loss

Book 2 has two different kinds of gear trouble, and they are not interchangeable. Mixing them up is the easiest way to corrupt your inventory.

Sometimes your Staff and Backpack are **taken temporarily**. In that case, the Assistant stores the gear and can return it when the recovery section arrives.

Other times, the book forces you to **abandon Backpack contents**. That's different. If the Backpack is truly discarded, do not expect it to come back later like captured gear.

The short version: temporary capture is a pause. Backpack discard is a loss. When the Assistant tells you which one is happening, believe it.

## Combat and Rolls

Use the Combat tab. Book 2 has enough special fights — Swamp Giant, Magdi, Scree Wyrm, the bridge sequences, the city encounters — that tracking modifiers by hand is asking for trouble. The presets exist for a reason.

Use the roll helper too. A random-number section is much easier when the app shows the possible results right next to the roll, and Book 2 throws random checks at you constantly.

If you're chasing combat achievements like **Swamp Giant Felled**, **Magdi Breaker**, or **Scree Wyrm Slayer**, save those for a replay. A first clear is about finishing the book, not picking every fight on the map.

## Common Mistakes

The first mistake is spending Willpower early because the choice looks exciting. Book 2 keeps asking for more. Save some.

The second mistake is treating every side route like it belongs in your first win. It does not. A focused run is stronger than a cluttered one, every time.

The third mistake is confusing captured gear with discarded gear. If the Assistant says your gear is stored, wait for the return. If the book says your Backpack is gone, believe it.

The fourth mistake is forgetting healing and protection items right up until you need them. Book 2 punishes messy inventory habits more than Book 1 ever did.

## Achievement Cleanup

Handle achievements in passes, not one cluttered megarun. First, **complete the book** and open the Shadow Gate. Get the win on the board. Second, do an **item run** aimed at Chaksu Pipes, Silver Knife, Karmo Potion, Black Rod, Mind Gem, and Azawood Leaves. Third, do a **story and combat run** chasing Karnali, Sado, Samu, Deathgaunt protection, Bareback Escape, Bridge Survivor, Swamp Giant, Magdi, and Scree Wyrm.

Trying to force all of that into one run will make the book feel messy. Give each pass a job.

For the structured single-sweep plan that gets Book 2 to 25/25 in one run, see the [100% Achievement Guide](Achievement-100-Percent-Guide). It's mostly about *not* skipping the side routes that look optional.

## Final Word

*The Forbidden City* rewards patience. It's bigger and harsher than Book 1, but it's fairer when you let each run have a purpose. For the first clear, protect Willpower and get to Section 310. For replay, pick the path you want and enjoy the hunt. That's where Book 2 stops feeling like survival and starts feeling like mastery.

Two weeks, two hundred miles, one Shadow Gate beneath a full moon. *Little time, much to do.* Get moving.
